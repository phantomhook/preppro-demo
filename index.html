Pillar 1 · CA Insurance Code & Ethics · Lesson 3

Prohibited Practices & Penalties

California's Insurance Code strictly regulates agent conduct to protect consumers and maintain market integrity. Violating these rules can result in license suspension, fines up to $10,000 per violation, and even criminal prosecution.

🚫 Key Prohibited Practices

  • Misrepresentation: Making false or misleading statements about a policy's benefits, terms, or an insurer's financial condition to induce a sale.
  • Twisting: Inducing a policyholder to lapse, surrender, or replace an existing policy through misrepresentation — primarily in life insurance.
  • Rebating: Offering anything of value not specified in the policy as an inducement to purchase insurance (e.g., cash back, gift cards, premium discounts).
  • Commingling: Mixing client premium funds with an agent's personal or business funds. All client funds must be held in a separate trust account.
  • Churning: Replacing a client's own policy (same insurer) unnecessarily to generate new commissions.

⚠️ Penalties Under CA Insurance Code

The California Department of Insurance (CDI) can take disciplinary action including license revocation, civil fines, and referral to the DA for criminal charges. Unlicensed activity is a misdemeanor (first offense) or felony (repeat/fraud).

📝 Quick Tip for the Exam

Distinguish twisting (replacement from one insurer to another) from churning (replacement within the same insurer). Both involve misrepresentation — twisting applies across insurers, churning applies within the same company.
